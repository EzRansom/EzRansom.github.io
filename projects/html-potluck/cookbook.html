Simple White Cake

Time: 40 mins    Author: Scottosman    Servings: 12

Ingredients

  • 1 cup white sugar
  • ½ cup unsalted butter
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 teaspoons vanilla extract
  • 1 ½ cups all-purpose flour
  • 1 ¾ teaspoons baking powder
  • ½ cup milk

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients. Preheat the oven to 350 degrees F (175 degrees C). Grease and flour a 9-inch square cake pan.
  2. Beat sugar and butter together in a mixing bowl with an electric mixer until lighter in color and fluffy. Add eggs, one at a time, beating briefly after each addition. Mix in vanilla.
  3. Combine flour and baking powder in a separate bowl. Add flour mixture to wet ingredients in batches, alternating with milk, beating cake batter briefly after each addition.
  4. Pour cake batter into the prepared pan.
  5. Bake cake in the preheated oven until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean, about 30 to 40 minutes.
  6. Remove from the oven and cool completely. Frost and enjoy!




Apple Funnel Cake

Time: 1 Hour    Author: Kathryn Hendrix, RDN, LD    Servings: 9


Ingredients

Batter

  • 2 1/2 cups all-purpose flour
  • 2 teaspoons baking powder
  • 1 teaspoon apple pie spice
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt
  • 1 3/4 cups milk
  • 1 cup applesauce
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/4 cup white sugar
  • 1/4 teaspoon almond extract

Apple Topper

  • 1/4 cup butter
  • 2 pounds apples, peeled and chopped (about 4 cups)
  • 1/3 cup packed brown sugar
  • 2 teaspoons apple pie spice

Fry and Serve

  • 2 cups vegetable oil, or as needed, for frying
  • caramel-flavor ice cream topping, and/or powdered sugar, for serving

Directions

  1. Stir together flour, baking powder, apple pie spice, and salt in a bowl for the batter.
  2. Whisk together milk, applesauce, eggs, white sugar, and almond extract in a large bowl until combined. Add flour mixture and whisk until smooth. Batter should have the consistency of a cake batter and be thin enough to pass through a funnel.
  3. For apple topper, melt butter in a large skillet over medium heat. Add apples, brown sugar, and apple pie spice; cook, stirring occasionally, until apples are tender, 15 to 20 minutes. Remove from heat.
  4. Meanwhile, pour oil to a depth 1 1/2 to 2 inches in a deep, heavy- bottomed 8- to 10-inch skillet or deep fryer; heat to 375 degrees F (190 degrees C).
  5. Put your finger over bottom opening of a funnel; fill funnel with 1/2 cup batter. Holding funnel close to surface of oil, release batter into oil while making a circular motion. Fry until golden brown, 1 to 2 minutes. Use tongs and a wide spatula to carefully turn funnel cake. If you don’t have a funnel, you can use a squeeze bottle instead. Fry until golden, about 1 minute more. Drain on paper towels. Repeat with remaining batter. Keep finished funnel cakes warm in a 180 degrees F (82 degrees C) oven while preparing remaining funnel cakes.
  6. Top with apple topper. Serve with caramel topping and/or powdered sugar.




Strawberry Churro Roll UpsS

   Time: 25    Author: Barrett Heald    Servings: 4    Yield: 12

Ingredients

Batter

  • 12 slices Hawaiian, brioche, or white sandwich bread
  • 6 tablespoons cream cheese, softened (3 ounces)
  • 1/4 cup strawberry jelly
  • 2 large eggs
  • 2 tablespoons milk
  • 1/4 cup sugar
  • 1 teaspoon ground cinnamon
  • 2 tablespoons salted butter
  • strawberry syrup (optional)

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Trim crusts from bread and flatten each slice with a rolling pin until about 1/8-inch thick.
  3. Place 1 1/2 teaspoons of cream cheese and 1 teaspoon of jelly on one bread slice in a straight line about 1-inch away from one side of the bread.
  4. Starting with the side closest to the filling, roll the bread up and set aside, seam side down. Repeat with remaining bread, cream cheese, and jelly.
  5. In a shallow bowl whisk together eggs and milk. In another shallow bowl combine sugar and cinnamon.
  6. Melt 1 tablespoon of the butter in a 12-inch nonstick skillet over low heat, Coat bread rolls in egg mixture. Place 6 roll-ups in skillet, seam side down and cook until golden brown on all sides, turning occasionally, 4 to 5 minutes. Repeat with remaining butter and roll-ups.
  7. Transfer warm roll-ups to cinnamon-sugar mixture and roll to coat.
  8. Serve with strawberry syrup, if desired.
